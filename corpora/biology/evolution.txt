Evolution is the change in heritable characteristics of biological populations over successive generations.
Natural selection is the process where organisms with favorable traits survive and reproduce more successfully.
Charles Darwin and Alfred Russel Wallace independently proposed the theory of natural selection.
Genetic variation arises from mutations genetic recombination and gene flow between populations.
Mutations are random changes in DNA sequence that can be beneficial neutral or harmful.
Adaptation is a trait that increases an organism fitness in its specific environment.
Speciation is the formation of new distinct species through evolutionary processes.
Allopatric speciation occurs when populations are geographically isolated and diverge genetically.
Sympatric speciation occurs without geographic isolation often through polyploidy or ecological specialization.
Genetic drift is the random change in allele frequencies that has a larger effect in small populations.
The founder effect occurs when a small group establishes a new population with reduced genetic diversity.
A population bottleneck drastically reduces population size and genetic diversity.
Gene flow is the transfer of genetic material between populations through migration.
Convergent evolution produces similar traits in unrelated species facing similar environmental pressures.
Divergent evolution causes related species to develop different traits as they adapt to different environments.
Coevolution occurs when two or more species reciprocally influence each other evolution.
Homologous structures have a common evolutionary origin but may serve different functions.
Analogous structures serve similar functions but have different evolutionary origins.
Vestigial structures are remnants of organs that were functional in ancestral species.
The fossil record provides direct evidence of evolutionary change over millions of years.
Molecular phylogenetics uses DNA and protein sequences to reconstruct evolutionary relationships.
The molecular clock hypothesis uses mutation rates to estimate the time of divergence between species.
Sexual selection drives the evolution of traits that increase mating success.
Kin selection explains altruistic behavior through the concept of inclusive fitness.
Endosymbiotic theory explains that mitochondria and chloroplasts originated from ancient prokaryotic organisms.
Mass extinctions have dramatically reduced biodiversity five major times in Earth history.
The Cambrian explosion approximately 541 million years ago saw the rapid diversification of most animal phyla.
Punctuated equilibrium proposes that evolution occurs in rapid bursts separated by long periods of stasis.
Phylogenetic trees represent the evolutionary relationships among groups of organisms.
Hardy-Weinberg equilibrium describes a population that is not evolving when five conditions are met.
