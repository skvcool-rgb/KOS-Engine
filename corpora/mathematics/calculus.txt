Calculus is the mathematical study of continuous change developed independently by Newton and Leibniz.
A limit describes the value that a function approaches as the input approaches a specified value.
The derivative of a function measures the instantaneous rate of change at any point.
The derivative of position with respect to time gives velocity.
The derivative of velocity with respect to time gives acceleration.
The power rule states that the derivative of x to the n equals n times x to the n minus 1.
The chain rule computes the derivative of a composite function as the product of derivatives.
The product rule states that the derivative of f times g equals f prime times g plus f times g prime.
The quotient rule computes the derivative of f divided by g.
A critical point occurs where the derivative equals zero or is undefined.
The second derivative test determines whether a critical point is a maximum minimum or inflection point.
Integration is the reverse process of differentiation and computes the area under a curve.
The fundamental theorem of calculus connects differentiation and integration.
A definite integral computes the net area between a function and the x-axis over an interval.
An indefinite integral represents a family of antiderivatives.
Integration by parts is based on the product rule and integrates products of functions.
Substitution simplifies integrals by changing the variable of integration.
Taylor series represent functions as infinite sums of polynomial terms based on derivatives at a point.
The Maclaurin series is a Taylor series centered at zero.
L Hopital rule evaluates limits of indeterminate forms by differentiating the numerator and denominator.
Differential equations relate a function to its derivatives and model many physical phenomena.
Ordinary differential equations involve functions of a single variable and their derivatives.
Partial differential equations involve functions of multiple variables and their partial derivatives.
The heat equation models the diffusion of heat through a material over time.
The wave equation describes the propagation of waves in a medium.
Laplace equation describes steady-state solutions where the second derivatives sum to zero.
Multivariable calculus extends differentiation and integration to functions of several variables.
The gradient is a vector of partial derivatives that points in the direction of steepest increase.
The divergence of a vector field measures the rate at which field lines spread out from a point.
The curl of a vector field measures the rotation of the field at a point.
