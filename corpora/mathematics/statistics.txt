Statistics is the science of collecting analyzing interpreting and presenting data.
Descriptive statistics summarize data using measures like mean median mode and standard deviation.
The mean is the arithmetic average of a dataset.
The median is the middle value when data is sorted and is robust to outliers.
Standard deviation measures the spread of data around the mean.
Variance is the square of the standard deviation and measures data dispersion.
The normal distribution is a bell-shaped probability distribution defined by its mean and standard deviation.
The central limit theorem states that the sum of many independent random variables approaches a normal distribution.
A confidence interval estimates a population parameter with a specified probability of containing the true value.
A 95 percent confidence interval means that if the sampling were repeated the interval would contain the true parameter 95 percent of the time.
Hypothesis testing evaluates whether sample data provides sufficient evidence against a null hypothesis.
The p-value is the probability of observing results at least as extreme as the data assuming the null hypothesis is true.
Type I error occurs when the null hypothesis is rejected when it is actually true also called a false positive.
Type II error occurs when the null hypothesis is not rejected when it is actually false also called a false negative.
The t-test compares the means of two groups to determine if they are statistically different.
ANOVA analysis of variance tests for differences in means among three or more groups.
Chi-squared test evaluates whether observed frequencies differ significantly from expected frequencies.
Regression analysis models the relationship between dependent and independent variables.
Linear regression fits a straight line to data minimizing the sum of squared residuals.
The coefficient of determination R-squared measures the proportion of variance in the dependent variable explained by the model.
Correlation measures the strength and direction of the linear relationship between two variables.
Correlation does not imply causation as two variables may be associated without one causing the other.
Bayes theorem calculates the probability of an event given prior knowledge using the formula P(A|B) equals P(B|A) times P(A) divided by P(B).
Bayesian statistics updates probability estimates as new evidence is obtained.
The law of large numbers states that as sample size increases the sample mean converges to the population mean.
Random sampling ensures every member of a population has an equal chance of being selected.
Bootstrapping estimates the sampling distribution of a statistic by resampling with replacement from the data.
The maximum likelihood method estimates parameters by finding values that maximize the probability of the observed data.
Principal component analysis reduces data dimensionality while preserving the maximum variance.
Monte Carlo simulation uses repeated random sampling to estimate complex mathematical quantities.
