Electrical engineering designs and develops systems that generate transmit and use electrical energy.
Alternating current AC changes direction periodically and is used for power transmission.
Direct current DC flows in one direction and is used in batteries and electronic circuits.
Transformers change AC voltage levels using electromagnetic induction between coupled coils.
Power grids transmit electricity at high voltage to reduce transmission losses.
Semiconductors have electrical conductivity between conductors and insulators and include silicon and germanium.
A diode allows current to flow in only one direction and is made from a p-n semiconductor junction.
A transistor is a semiconductor device that amplifies or switches electronic signals.
MOSFET metal-oxide-semiconductor field-effect transistor is the most widely used transistor type in integrated circuits.
Integrated circuits combine billions of transistors on a single silicon chip.
Moore law observed that the number of transistors on a chip roughly doubles every two years.
Operational amplifiers are versatile analog circuits used for amplification filtering and signal conditioning.
Analog-to-digital converters transform continuous signals into discrete digital values.
Digital-to-analog converters transform discrete digital values back into continuous signals.
Boolean algebra is the mathematical foundation of digital logic using AND OR and NOT operations.
Flip-flops are digital circuits that store one bit of data and form the basis of registers and memory.
FPGA field-programmable gate arrays are reconfigurable integrated circuits for custom digital logic.
Microprocessors execute instructions from computer programs using an arithmetic logic unit and control unit.
Embedded systems are specialized computer systems designed to perform dedicated functions within larger devices.
Signal processing analyzes modifies and synthesizes signals such as sound images and sensor data.
The Fourier transform decomposes signals into their constituent frequencies.
The Nyquist theorem states that a signal must be sampled at twice its highest frequency to be accurately reconstructed.
Control systems use feedback to maintain desired output by adjusting inputs.
PID controllers use proportional integral and derivative terms to minimize error in control systems.
Power electronics convert and control electrical power using semiconductor switching devices.
Solar photovoltaic cells convert sunlight directly into electricity using the photovoltaic effect.
Lithium-ion batteries store energy through reversible lithium ion movement between electrodes.
Supercapacitors store energy electrostatically and can charge and discharge much faster than batteries.
Wireless communication transmits information through electromagnetic waves without physical conductors.
5G networks use millimeter wave frequencies and massive MIMO to achieve higher data rates and lower latency.
