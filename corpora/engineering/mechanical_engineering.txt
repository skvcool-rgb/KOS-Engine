Mechanical engineering applies physics and materials science to design build and maintain mechanical systems.
Stress is the internal force per unit area within a material caused by external loads.
Strain is the deformation of a material relative to its original dimensions.
Young modulus relates stress to strain in the elastic region of a material.
The yield strength is the stress at which a material begins to deform permanently.
Fatigue failure occurs when a material fractures after repeated cyclic loading below its ultimate strength.
Fluid mechanics studies the behavior of liquids and gases at rest and in motion.
Bernoulli equation relates fluid pressure velocity and elevation along a streamline.
Reynolds number determines whether fluid flow is laminar smooth or turbulent chaotic.
Viscosity measures a fluid resistance to flow and depends on temperature.
Heat exchangers transfer thermal energy between two fluids without mixing them.
Thermodynamic cycles like the Rankine and Brayton cycles describe the operation of power plants and gas turbines.
The Otto cycle describes the idealized operation of spark-ignition internal combustion engines.
The Diesel cycle describes compression-ignition engines that have higher efficiency than Otto cycle engines.
Gear trains transmit rotational motion and torque between shafts using meshing gears.
Bearings reduce friction between moving parts by providing a smooth rolling or sliding surface.
Vibration analysis identifies resonance frequencies and prevents structural failure from oscillations.
Finite element analysis divides structures into small elements to numerically solve stress and deformation problems.
Computational fluid dynamics simulates fluid flow using numerical methods to solve the Navier-Stokes equations.
3D printing or additive manufacturing builds objects layer by layer from digital models.
CNC computer numerical control machining uses programmed instructions to precisely cut material.
Robotics combines mechanical engineering electronics and software to create autonomous or semi-autonomous machines.
Industrial robots perform repetitive tasks like welding painting and assembly with high precision.
HVAC systems control heating ventilation and air conditioning in buildings.
Turbines convert fluid energy into mechanical energy and are used in power generation and jet engines.
Composite materials combine two or more constituent materials to achieve properties not available from individual materials.
Carbon fiber composites are lightweight and strong and used in aerospace and automotive applications.
Hydraulic systems use pressurized fluid to transmit force and perform mechanical work.
Pneumatic systems use compressed air to transmit force for tools and automation.
Quality control uses statistical methods to ensure products meet specified standards and tolerances.
