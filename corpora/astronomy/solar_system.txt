The solar system formed approximately 4.6 billion years ago from a collapsing cloud of gas and dust.
The Sun contains 99.86 percent of the total mass of the solar system.
The Sun is a G-type main-sequence star that fuses hydrogen into helium in its core.
The Sun core temperature is approximately 15 million degrees Celsius.
Mercury is the smallest planet and closest to the Sun with extreme temperature variations.
Venus has a dense carbon dioxide atmosphere with surface temperatures exceeding 460 degrees Celsius.
Earth is the only known planet to support liquid water on its surface and harbor life.
Mars has the largest volcano in the solar system Olympus Mons at 21.9 kilometers high.
The asteroid belt lies between Mars and Jupiter and contains millions of rocky bodies.
Ceres is the largest object in the asteroid belt and is classified as a dwarf planet.
Jupiter is the largest planet with a mass more than twice that of all other planets combined.
Jupiter Great Red Spot is a storm that has persisted for at least 350 years.
Saturn is known for its extensive ring system made primarily of ice particles and rocky debris.
Titan Saturn largest moon has a dense nitrogen atmosphere and liquid methane lakes on its surface.
Enceladus Saturn moon has geysers of water ice suggesting a subsurface ocean.
Uranus rotates on its side with an axial tilt of approximately 98 degrees.
Neptune has the strongest sustained winds in the solar system reaching 2100 kilometers per hour.
Pluto was reclassified as a dwarf planet in 2006 by the International Astronomical Union.
The Kuiper Belt is a region beyond Neptune containing icy bodies including Pluto and Eris.
The Oort Cloud is a theoretical spherical shell of icy objects surrounding the solar system at up to 100000 AU.
Comets are icy bodies that develop tails of gas and dust when they approach the Sun.
The Moon formed approximately 4.5 billion years ago likely from debris after a Mars-sized body collided with early Earth.
Tides on Earth are primarily caused by the gravitational pull of the Moon and to a lesser extent the Sun.
Solar wind is a stream of charged particles flowing from the Sun corona at 400 to 800 kilometers per second.
The heliosphere is the bubble of solar wind that extends beyond the orbit of Pluto.
Voyager 1 launched in 1977 entered interstellar space in 2012 becoming the first human-made object to do so.
Europa Jupiter moon has a subsurface ocean beneath its ice crust and is a candidate for extraterrestrial life.
The habitable zone is the region around a star where liquid water can exist on a planet surface.
Meteorites are fragments of asteroids or comets that survive passage through Earth atmosphere.
The solar cycle lasts approximately 11 years and includes periods of maximum and minimum sunspot activity.
