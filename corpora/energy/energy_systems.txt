Solar photovoltaic cells convert sunlight into electricity using semiconductor materials primarily silicon.
Solar cell efficiency has improved from 6 percent in 1954 to over 47 percent in multi-junction laboratory cells.
Perovskite solar cells are an emerging technology with rapidly improving efficiency and low manufacturing costs.
Wind turbines convert kinetic energy of wind into electrical energy using rotating blades and generators.
Onshore wind turbines typically have capacities of 2 to 5 megawatts per turbine.
Offshore wind farms benefit from stronger and more consistent winds but face higher installation costs.
Hydroelectric power generates electricity from the potential energy of water flowing through turbines.
Hydroelectric dams provide approximately 16 percent of global electricity production.
Pumped-storage hydroelectricity stores energy by pumping water uphill during low demand and releasing it during peak demand.
Nuclear fission splits heavy atoms like uranium-235 to release enormous amounts of energy.
A nuclear reactor controls the fission chain reaction using control rods and moderators.
Nuclear power plants produce no direct carbon dioxide emissions during operation.
Nuclear fusion combines light atoms like hydrogen isotopes to release energy following E equals mc squared.
ITER is an international project building a tokamak fusion reactor to demonstrate net fusion energy.
Geothermal energy extracts heat from Earth interior for electricity generation and direct heating.
Biomass energy comes from organic materials including wood agricultural waste and dedicated energy crops.
Hydrogen can be produced by electrolysis of water using renewable electricity and used as a clean fuel.
Fuel cells convert hydrogen and oxygen directly into electricity water and heat.
Battery energy storage systems use rechargeable batteries to store electricity for later use.
Lithium-ion batteries dominate the energy storage market due to high energy density and declining costs.
Solid-state batteries replace liquid electrolytes with solid materials for improved safety and energy density.
Grid-scale energy storage is essential for integrating intermittent renewable energy sources.
Smart grids use digital communication technology to detect and react to local changes in electricity usage.
The levelized cost of energy measures the average cost of electricity generation over a project lifetime.
Solar and wind energy have achieved cost parity with fossil fuels in many regions worldwide.
Carbon capture utilization and storage removes CO2 from industrial emissions or the atmosphere.
Direct air capture technology removes carbon dioxide directly from ambient air.
Combined heat and power systems generate electricity and useful thermal energy simultaneously.
Superconducting magnetic energy storage uses superconducting coils to store energy in a magnetic field.
The global energy transition aims to shift from fossil fuels to renewable and clean energy sources.
