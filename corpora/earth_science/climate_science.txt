Climate is the long-term average of weather patterns in a region typically measured over 30 years.
Weather is the short-term state of the atmosphere including temperature humidity precipitation and wind.
The greenhouse effect traps heat in the atmosphere when greenhouse gases absorb and re-emit infrared radiation.
Carbon dioxide CO2 is the primary greenhouse gas driving current climate change from fossil fuel combustion.
Methane CH4 is a greenhouse gas approximately 80 times more potent than CO2 over a 20-year period.
Nitrous oxide N2O is a greenhouse gas released from agriculture and industrial processes.
Global average temperature has increased approximately 1.1 degrees Celsius since the pre-industrial era.
The Paris Agreement aims to limit global warming to 1.5 degrees Celsius above pre-industrial levels.
Arctic sea ice has declined by approximately 13 percent per decade since satellite records began in 1979.
Sea level is rising at approximately 3.6 millimeters per year due to thermal expansion and ice sheet melting.
The Greenland and Antarctic ice sheets contain enough ice to raise sea level by approximately 65 meters if fully melted.
Ocean acidification occurs as the ocean absorbs CO2 lowering pH and threatening marine ecosystems.
The thermohaline circulation is a global ocean conveyor belt driven by differences in temperature and salinity.
El Nino Southern Oscillation is a periodic climate pattern that affects weather worldwide.
The Milankovitch cycles are variations in Earth orbit that influence long-term climate patterns over thousands of years.
Albedo is the fraction of incoming solar radiation reflected by a surface with ice having high albedo and ocean having low albedo.
Positive feedback loops amplify climate changes such as ice-albedo feedback and permafrost thawing.
Carbon sinks absorb more carbon than they release and include forests oceans and soil.
Deforestation releases stored carbon and reduces the land carbon sink capacity.
Coral bleaching occurs when ocean temperatures rise causing corals to expel their symbiotic algae.
The Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change IPCC synthesizes climate science for policymakers.
Climate models simulate Earth climate system using mathematical equations based on physics.
Renewable energy sources produce electricity without direct greenhouse gas emissions.
Carbon capture and storage technology removes CO2 from emissions or the atmosphere and stores it underground.
The ozone layer in the stratosphere absorbs harmful ultraviolet radiation from the Sun.
The Montreal Protocol successfully reduced the production of ozone-depleting substances like CFCs.
Urban heat islands cause cities to be several degrees warmer than surrounding rural areas.
Extreme weather events including heatwaves droughts and hurricanes are intensifying due to climate change.
The cryosphere encompasses all frozen water on Earth including glaciers ice sheets and permafrost.
Atmospheric CO2 concentration has risen from 280 parts per million pre-industrial to over 420 parts per million.
