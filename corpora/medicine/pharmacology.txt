Pharmacology is the study of how drugs interact with biological systems to produce therapeutic effects.
Pharmacokinetics describes how the body absorbs distributes metabolizes and excretes drugs.
Pharmacodynamics describes how drugs produce their effects at the molecular and cellular level.
Drug bioavailability is the fraction of an administered dose that reaches systemic circulation.
The therapeutic index is the ratio of the toxic dose to the therapeutic dose and measures drug safety.
Receptor agonists bind to receptors and activate them to produce a biological response.
Receptor antagonists bind to receptors and block the natural ligand from activating them.
Antibiotics kill or inhibit the growth of bacteria and include penicillins cephalosporins and fluoroquinolones.
Penicillin discovered by Alexander Fleming in 1928 inhibits bacterial cell wall synthesis.
Antibiotic resistance occurs when bacteria evolve mechanisms to survive antibiotic exposure.
MRSA methicillin-resistant Staphylococcus aureus is a serious antibiotic-resistant infection.
Antiviral drugs inhibit viral replication and include acyclovir for herpes and oseltamivir for influenza.
Nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drugs NSAIDs like ibuprofen reduce inflammation by inhibiting cyclooxygenase enzymes.
Opioid analgesics like morphine bind to mu receptors in the brain to produce pain relief and euphoria.
Statins lower cholesterol by inhibiting HMG-CoA reductase the enzyme in cholesterol biosynthesis.
ACE inhibitors lower blood pressure by blocking the conversion of angiotensin I to angiotensin II.
Beta blockers reduce heart rate and blood pressure by blocking beta-adrenergic receptors.
Selective serotonin reuptake inhibitors SSRIs treat depression by increasing serotonin levels in synapses.
Immunosuppressants like cyclosporine prevent organ transplant rejection by suppressing T cell activation.
Monoclonal antibodies are engineered proteins that target specific molecules for cancer therapy and autoimmune diseases.
Chemotherapy drugs kill rapidly dividing cells and are used to treat cancer.
Drug-drug interactions occur when one drug affects the pharmacokinetics or pharmacodynamics of another.
The cytochrome P450 enzyme system in the liver metabolizes the majority of pharmaceutical drugs.
Prodrugs are inactive compounds that are metabolized into active drugs inside the body.
The blood-brain barrier limits drug delivery to the central nervous system.
Pharmacogenomics studies how genetic variation affects individual responses to drugs.
Drug tolerance develops when repeated use reduces the drug effect requiring higher doses.
Physical dependence occurs when the body adapts to a drug and withdrawal symptoms appear upon cessation.
Clinical trials test drug safety and efficacy through phase I II III and IV studies.
The FDA approves drugs for market after reviewing safety and efficacy data from clinical trials.
