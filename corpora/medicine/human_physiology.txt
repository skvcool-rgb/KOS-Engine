The cardiovascular system consists of the heart blood vessels and blood which transports oxygen nutrients and waste.
The heart has four chambers two atria and two ventricles that pump blood through pulmonary and systemic circuits.
Blood pressure is measured as systolic over diastolic with normal being approximately 120 over 80 mmHg.
Red blood cells contain hemoglobin which binds oxygen in the lungs and releases it in tissues.
White blood cells are immune cells that defend the body against infections and foreign substances.
Platelets are cell fragments that initiate blood clotting at sites of vascular injury.
The respiratory system exchanges oxygen and carbon dioxide between the air and blood in the alveoli.
The diaphragm is the primary muscle of respiration that contracts to expand the lungs during inhalation.
Gas exchange in the lungs occurs by diffusion across the thin walls of alveoli and capillaries.
The digestive system breaks down food into nutrients through mechanical and chemical processes.
The stomach produces hydrochloric acid and pepsin to digest proteins.
The small intestine is the primary site of nutrient absorption with villi and microvilli increasing surface area.
The liver produces bile detoxifies chemicals and metabolizes drugs and nutrients.
The pancreas produces digestive enzymes and the hormones insulin and glucagon.
The kidneys filter blood to remove waste products and regulate fluid and electrolyte balance.
Nephrons are the functional units of the kidney that filter blood and produce urine.
The nervous system consists of the central nervous system brain and spinal cord and peripheral nervous system.
Action potentials are electrical signals that travel along nerve cell axons at speeds up to 120 meters per second.
Neurotransmitters are chemical messengers released at synapses to transmit signals between neurons.
The endocrine system uses hormones secreted by glands to regulate metabolism growth and reproduction.
Insulin is produced by beta cells in the pancreas and lowers blood glucose levels.
Glucagon is produced by alpha cells and raises blood glucose levels.
The thyroid gland produces thyroxine which regulates metabolic rate.
The adrenal glands produce cortisol for stress response and aldosterone for sodium balance.
The immune system has innate and adaptive components that work together to defend against pathogens.
Antibodies are proteins produced by B cells that bind to specific antigens on pathogens.
T cells include helper cells that coordinate immune responses and killer cells that destroy infected cells.
Vaccination stimulates the adaptive immune system to produce memory cells without causing disease.
The musculoskeletal system provides support movement and protection through bones muscles and joints.
Bone is living tissue that is constantly remodeled by osteoblasts building bone and osteoclasts breaking it down.
