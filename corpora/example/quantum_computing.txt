A qubit is the fundamental unit of quantum computing.
Unlike classical bits, a qubit can exist in superposition of 0 and 1 simultaneously.
Entanglement links two qubits so measuring one instantly determines the other.
Quantum gates manipulate qubits similar to how logic gates manipulate bits.
The Hadamard gate puts a qubit into equal superposition of 0 and 1.
The CNOT gate entangles two qubits creating a controlled-NOT operation.
Decoherence destroys quantum states when qubits interact with the environment.
Error correction in quantum computing requires many physical qubits per logical qubit.
Shor algorithm factors large numbers exponentially faster than classical algorithms.
Grover algorithm searches unsorted databases quadratically faster than classical search.
IBM and Google are leading companies building superconducting quantum processors.
Trapped ion quantum computers use individual atoms confined by electromagnetic fields.
Quantum supremacy means a quantum computer solves a problem no classical computer can solve in reasonable time.
Quantum annealing is a specialized approach used by D-Wave for optimization problems.
Topological qubits use anyons to store information in a way that resists decoherence.
