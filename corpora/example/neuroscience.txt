Neurons are the fundamental cells of the nervous system that transmit electrical signals.
Synapses are junctions between neurons where neurotransmitters cross the synaptic cleft.
Dopamine is a neurotransmitter involved in reward motivation and motor control.
Serotonin regulates mood sleep appetite and is targeted by antidepressant medications.
The hippocampus is critical for forming new long-term memories.
The amygdala processes emotional responses especially fear and threat detection.
The prefrontal cortex handles executive functions planning and decision making.
Neuroplasticity allows the brain to reorganize neural pathways based on experience.
Long-term potentiation strengthens synaptic connections through repeated stimulation.
Myelin sheaths insulate axons and increase the speed of electrical signal transmission.
Glial cells support neurons by providing nutrients and removing waste.
The blood-brain barrier protects the brain from harmful substances in the bloodstream.
Mirror neurons fire both when performing an action and when observing another perform it.
Sleep consolidation transfers memories from hippocampus to cortex during slow-wave sleep.
The default mode network activates during rest and is involved in self-referential thinking.
