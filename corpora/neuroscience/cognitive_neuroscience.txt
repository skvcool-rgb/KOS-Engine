Cognitive neuroscience studies the neural mechanisms underlying cognitive functions like perception memory and language.
Working memory temporarily holds and manipulates information and depends on the prefrontal cortex.
Long-term memory is stored across distributed cortical networks and can last a lifetime.
Declarative memory includes episodic memories of personal experiences and semantic memories of facts.
Procedural memory stores motor skills and habits and is mediated by the basal ganglia and cerebellum.
Memory consolidation transfers information from short-term hippocampal storage to long-term cortical storage during sleep.
The hippocampus is essential for spatial navigation and forming new explicit memories.
Place cells in the hippocampus fire when an animal is in a specific location in its environment.
Grid cells in the entorhinal cortex fire in regular hexagonal patterns as an animal moves through space.
The visual cortex processes visual information in a hierarchical manner from simple to complex features.
The primary visual cortex V1 processes edges orientations and basic visual features.
Face recognition involves the fusiform face area a specialized region in the temporal lobe.
Broca area in the left frontal lobe is essential for speech production and language processing.
Wernicke area in the left temporal lobe is critical for language comprehension.
Attention selectively enhances processing of relevant information while suppressing irrelevant stimuli.
The reticular activating system in the brainstem regulates wakefulness and arousal.
Emotion processing involves the amygdala prefrontal cortex and insula working together.
The reward system uses dopamine pathways from the ventral tegmental area to the nucleus accumbens.
Executive functions including planning inhibition and cognitive flexibility depend on the prefrontal cortex.
Consciousness remains one of the greatest unsolved problems in neuroscience and philosophy.
The global workspace theory proposes that consciousness arises when information is broadcast widely across the brain.
Integrated information theory proposes that consciousness corresponds to the amount of integrated information in a system.
Brain-computer interfaces decode neural activity to allow direct communication between the brain and external devices.
Functional MRI measures brain activity by detecting changes in blood oxygenation levels.
Electroencephalography EEG measures electrical activity of the brain with millisecond temporal resolution.
Optogenetics uses light-sensitive proteins to activate or inhibit specific neurons with precise timing.
The connectome is the complete map of neural connections in the brain.
Neurogenesis the generation of new neurons occurs in the hippocampus and olfactory bulb of adult mammals.
Traumatic brain injury can cause cognitive impairments depending on the location and severity of damage.
Neurodegenerative diseases progressively destroy neurons leading to cognitive and motor decline.
