Organic chemistry is the study of carbon-containing compounds and their reactions.
Carbon can form four covalent bonds due to its four valence electrons.
Hydrocarbons contain only carbon and hydrogen atoms.
Alkanes are saturated hydrocarbons with single bonds following the formula CnH2n+2.
Alkenes contain at least one carbon-carbon double bond and are unsaturated.
Alkynes contain at least one carbon-carbon triple bond.
Aromatic compounds contain a planar ring of atoms with delocalized pi electrons such as benzene.
Benzene has six carbon atoms in a hexagonal ring with alternating single and double bonds that are actually delocalized.
Functional groups determine the chemical properties and reactivity of organic molecules.
The hydroxyl group OH characterizes alcohols.
The carboxyl group COOH characterizes carboxylic acids.
The amino group NH2 characterizes amines and is found in amino acids.
The carbonyl group C double bond O is found in aldehydes and ketones.
Esters form from the reaction of a carboxylic acid with an alcohol releasing water.
Polymers are large molecules made by linking many small monomer units together.
Addition polymerization joins monomers with double bonds without losing atoms.
Condensation polymerization joins monomers by removing small molecules like water.
Chirality describes molecules that are non-superimposable mirror images of each other called enantiomers.
Enantiomers have identical physical properties except for the direction they rotate polarized light.
Stereoisomers have the same molecular formula and connectivity but different spatial arrangements.
Nucleophilic substitution reactions replace a leaving group with a nucleophile.
SN1 reactions proceed through a carbocation intermediate and follow first-order kinetics.
SN2 reactions proceed through a single concerted step with backside attack and follow second-order kinetics.
Elimination reactions remove atoms from adjacent carbons to form a double bond.
Grignard reagents are organomagnesium compounds used to form carbon-carbon bonds.
Friedel-Crafts reactions add alkyl or acyl groups to aromatic rings using Lewis acid catalysts.
Oxidation of primary alcohols produces aldehydes and further oxidation produces carboxylic acids.
Reduction adds hydrogen atoms to a molecule or removes oxygen atoms.
Markovnikov rule states that in addition reactions the hydrogen adds to the carbon with more hydrogen atoms.
Isomers are molecules with the same molecular formula but different structural arrangements.
