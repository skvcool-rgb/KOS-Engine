The zeroth law of thermodynamics states that if two systems are each in thermal equilibrium with a third they are in thermal equilibrium with each other.
The first law of thermodynamics states that energy is conserved and heat added to a system equals internal energy change plus work done by the system.
The second law of thermodynamics states that entropy of an isolated system never decreases over time.
The third law of thermodynamics states that the entropy of a perfect crystal approaches zero as temperature approaches absolute zero.
Entropy is a measure of the number of microscopic configurations consistent with the macroscopic state.
Heat always flows spontaneously from a hotter body to a cooler body never the reverse without external work.
The Carnot cycle defines the maximum efficiency possible for a heat engine operating between two temperatures.
Carnot efficiency equals one minus the ratio of cold reservoir temperature to hot reservoir temperature.
Specific heat capacity is the energy required to raise one kilogram of a substance by one degree Celsius.
Latent heat is the energy absorbed or released during a phase transition at constant temperature.
The ideal gas law states that pressure times volume equals the number of moles times the gas constant times temperature.
Boyle law states that at constant temperature the pressure of a gas is inversely proportional to its volume.
Charles law states that at constant pressure the volume of a gas is directly proportional to its temperature.
The Boltzmann constant relates the average kinetic energy of particles to the temperature of the gas.
The Maxwell-Boltzmann distribution describes the speeds of particles in an ideal gas.
An adiabatic process occurs without heat exchange between the system and its surroundings.
An isothermal process occurs at constant temperature.
An isobaric process occurs at constant pressure.
An isochoric process occurs at constant volume.
Free energy determines whether a chemical reaction occurs spontaneously at constant temperature and pressure.
Gibbs free energy equals enthalpy minus temperature times entropy.
A heat pump moves thermal energy from a cold reservoir to a hot reservoir using external work.
The Stefan-Boltzmann law states that the total energy radiated by a black body is proportional to the fourth power of its temperature.
Thermal conductivity measures how well a material conducts heat.
Convection transfers heat through bulk fluid motion driven by density differences.
Radiation transfers heat through electromagnetic waves without requiring a medium.
Phase diagrams show the stable phases of a substance as a function of temperature and pressure.
The triple point is the unique temperature and pressure at which solid liquid and gas coexist in equilibrium.
Supercritical fluids form above the critical temperature and pressure where liquid and gas phases merge.
The equipartition theorem states that each quadratic degree of freedom contributes half kT to the average energy.
