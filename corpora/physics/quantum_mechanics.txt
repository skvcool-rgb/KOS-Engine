Quantum mechanics describes the behavior of matter and energy at the atomic and subatomic scale.
The wave-particle duality principle states that all matter exhibits both wave and particle properties.
The Heisenberg uncertainty principle states that position and momentum cannot both be precisely determined simultaneously.
The Schrodinger equation describes how the quantum state of a physical system evolves over time.
The wave function contains all information about a quantum system and its square gives the probability density.
Quantum superposition allows a system to exist in multiple states simultaneously until measured.
Measurement in quantum mechanics causes the wave function to collapse to a definite eigenstate.
The Pauli exclusion principle states that no two identical fermions can occupy the same quantum state.
Fermions have half-integer spin and include electrons protons and neutrons.
Bosons have integer spin and include photons gluons and the Higgs boson.
Quantum tunneling allows particles to pass through energy barriers that would be impenetrable classically.
The photoelectric effect demonstrated that light consists of quantized packets of energy called photons.
Planck constant relates the energy of a photon to its frequency as E equals h times frequency.
The de Broglie wavelength of a particle equals Planck constant divided by its momentum.
Quantum entanglement creates correlations between particles that persist regardless of distance.
The Bell inequalities show that quantum mechanics cannot be explained by local hidden variable theories.
The EPR paradox highlighted apparent conflicts between quantum mechanics and local realism.
Quantum decoherence explains the emergence of classical behavior from quantum systems through environmental interaction.
The hydrogen atom energy levels are quantized and given by the Bohr model as negative 13.6 eV divided by n squared.
Electron orbitals are described by quantum numbers n l m and s representing energy angular momentum magnetic moment and spin.
The Stern-Gerlach experiment demonstrated the quantization of angular momentum.
Quantum field theory combines quantum mechanics with special relativity to describe particle physics.
The standard model of particle physics classifies all known elementary particles and three of the four fundamental forces.
Quarks combine to form hadrons including protons and neutrons through the strong nuclear force.
Leptons include electrons muons taus and their associated neutrinos.
The strong nuclear force is mediated by gluons and binds quarks together inside protons and neutrons.
The weak nuclear force is responsible for radioactive beta decay and is mediated by W and Z bosons.
The Higgs mechanism gives mass to elementary particles through their interaction with the Higgs field.
The Higgs boson was discovered at CERN in 2012 confirming the existence of the Higgs field.
Quantum chromodynamics describes the strong interaction between quarks and gluons using color charge.
