Nuclear physics studies the constituents and interactions of atomic nuclei.
The strong nuclear force binds protons and neutrons together in the nucleus overcoming electromagnetic repulsion.
The binding energy per nucleon is highest for iron-56 making it the most stable nucleus.
Radioactive decay is the spontaneous emission of particles or radiation from an unstable nucleus.
Alpha decay emits a helium-4 nucleus reducing the atomic number by two and mass number by four.
Beta decay converts a neutron into a proton emitting an electron and antineutrino.
Gamma decay emits high-energy photons from an excited nucleus without changing its composition.
The half-life is the time required for half of a radioactive sample to decay.
Carbon-14 dating uses the known half-life of 5730 years to determine the age of organic materials.
Nuclear fission splits a heavy nucleus into lighter nuclei releasing energy and neutrons.
Uranium-235 is the primary fissile isotope used in nuclear reactors and weapons.
A chain reaction occurs when fission neutrons cause additional fission events in a self-sustaining process.
Control rods absorb neutrons to regulate the rate of fission in a nuclear reactor.
Nuclear fusion combines light nuclei like hydrogen isotopes to form heavier nuclei releasing energy.
The proton-proton chain is the primary fusion process powering the Sun.
Deuterium and tritium are hydrogen isotopes used in experimental fusion reactors.
Plasma is an ionized gas state of matter required for sustained fusion reactions.
Magnetic confinement uses strong magnetic fields to contain hot plasma in a tokamak or stellarator.
Inertial confinement uses intense lasers to compress and heat fuel pellets to achieve fusion.
Nuclear waste from fission includes spent fuel rods containing long-lived radioactive isotopes.
Breeder reactors produce more fissile material than they consume by converting fertile isotopes.
The nuclear shell model explains the stability of nuclei with magic numbers of protons or neutrons.
The liquid drop model treats the nucleus as a drop of incompressible nuclear fluid.
Neutron stars are composed almost entirely of neutrons and are among the densest objects in the universe.
Nuclear magnetic resonance uses the magnetic properties of nuclei for chemical analysis and medical imaging.
MRI magnetic resonance imaging creates detailed images of organs using nuclear magnetic resonance.
Particle accelerators accelerate charged particles to high energies for nuclear and particle physics experiments.
The Large Hadron Collider at CERN is the world largest particle accelerator at 27 kilometers in circumference.
Antimatter is composed of antiparticles with the same mass but opposite charge as normal matter.
When matter and antimatter meet they annihilate each other converting their mass entirely into energy.
