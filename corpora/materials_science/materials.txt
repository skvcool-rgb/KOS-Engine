Materials science studies the relationships between the structure properties processing and performance of materials.
Crystalline materials have atoms arranged in a regular repeating pattern called a crystal lattice.
Amorphous materials lack long-range atomic order and include glasses and some polymers.
Metals are characterized by high electrical and thermal conductivity ductility and metallic bonding.
Steel is an alloy of iron and carbon with properties that depend on carbon content and heat treatment.
Stainless steel contains chromium which forms a passive oxide layer that resists corrosion.
Aluminum alloys are lightweight and corrosion-resistant and widely used in aerospace and transportation.
Titanium alloys have high strength-to-weight ratio and excellent corrosion resistance.
Ceramics are hard brittle inorganic materials with high melting points and electrical insulation properties.
Silicon carbide and aluminum oxide are advanced ceramics used in cutting tools and armor.
Polymers are long-chain molecules made of repeating monomer units with diverse mechanical properties.
Thermoplastics soften when heated and can be remolded including polyethylene polypropylene and nylon.
Thermosets harden permanently when cured and cannot be remolded including epoxy and phenolic resins.
Elastomers are polymers with high elasticity that can stretch and return to their original shape.
Semiconductors have conductivity that increases with temperature and can be controlled by doping.
Doping introduces impurity atoms into a semiconductor to increase its conductivity.
N-type semiconductors have excess electrons from donor dopants like phosphorus in silicon.
P-type semiconductors have excess holes from acceptor dopants like boron in silicon.
Nanomaterials have at least one dimension smaller than 100 nanometers and exhibit unique properties.
Carbon nanotubes are cylindrical nanostructures with exceptional strength and electrical conductivity.
Graphene is a single layer of carbon atoms with remarkable strength conductivity and flexibility.
Biomaterials are designed to interact with biological systems for medical devices and implants.
Shape memory alloys like nitinol can return to a predetermined shape when heated.
Piezoelectric materials generate electric charge when mechanically stressed.
Superconductors exhibit zero electrical resistance below their critical temperature.
High-temperature superconductors operate at temperatures achievable with liquid nitrogen cooling.
Metamaterials are engineered materials with properties not found in nature such as negative refractive index.
Perovskites are a class of materials with a specific crystal structure showing promise for solar cells.
Corrosion is the degradation of metals through chemical reactions with their environment.
Fracture toughness measures a material resistance to crack propagation.
