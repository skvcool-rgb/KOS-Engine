Microeconomics studies individual agents and markets and how they make decisions about resource allocation.
The law of demand states that quantity demanded decreases as price increases holding other factors constant.
The law of supply states that quantity supplied increases as price increases holding other factors constant.
Market equilibrium occurs at the price where quantity demanded equals quantity supplied.
Price elasticity of demand measures how much quantity demanded changes in response to a price change.
Inelastic demand means quantity demanded changes less than proportionally to price changes.
Consumer surplus is the difference between what consumers are willing to pay and the market price.
Producer surplus is the difference between the market price and the minimum price producers would accept.
Marginal cost is the additional cost of producing one more unit of a good or service.
Marginal revenue is the additional revenue from selling one more unit of a good or service.
Firms maximize profit by producing where marginal revenue equals marginal cost.
Perfect competition features many firms selling identical products with no barriers to entry.
Monopoly exists when a single firm dominates a market with no close substitutes and barriers to entry.
Oligopoly is a market structure with few large firms whose decisions affect each other.
Game theory analyzes strategic interactions between rational decision-makers.
The Nash equilibrium is a strategy combination where no player can benefit by unilaterally changing strategy.
The prisoner dilemma shows how individual rationality can lead to collectively suboptimal outcomes.
Externalities are costs or benefits of a transaction that affect third parties not involved in the transaction.
Negative externalities like pollution cause social costs to exceed private costs.
A Pigouvian tax corrects negative externalities by taxing the activity equal to the marginal social cost.
Public goods are non-rivalrous and non-excludable meaning one person use does not reduce availability to others.
Moral hazard occurs when a party takes greater risks because another party bears the cost.
Adverse selection occurs when asymmetric information leads to market transactions favoring one party.
The tragedy of the commons describes how shared resources are overused when individuals act in self-interest.
Opportunity cost is the value of the next best alternative foregone when making a choice.
Diminishing marginal returns states that adding more of one input while holding others constant eventually yields less additional output.
Economies of scale occur when increasing production reduces the average cost per unit.
Price discrimination charges different prices to different consumers based on willingness to pay.
Behavioral economics incorporates psychological insights into economic models of decision-making.
The Coase theorem states that with low transaction costs parties can negotiate to correct externalities regardless of initial property rights.
